Gutter Guard Prices by Type

Not all gutter guards are created equal. The type you choose is the single biggest factor in what you'll pay. Here's what each category costs per linear foot, installed, in the Cherry Hill area:

Gutter Guard Type Cost Per Linear Foot (Installed) Effectiveness Lifespan
Screen / mesh inserts $3–$8 Low to moderate 3–7 years
Foam inserts $4–$8 Low 2–5 years
Reverse curve / surface tension $8–$18 Moderate 10–20 years
Micro-mesh (e.g., GutterGlove) $15–$28 High 20+ years (lifetime warranty)

Screen and Mesh Inserts ($3–$8/ft)

The budget option. These are basic metal or plastic screens that sit inside or on top of your gutter. They block large leaves but let through pine needles, seed pods, and shingle grit. You'll still need to clean your gutters — just less often. Most screen guards need replacing every few years as they rust, sag, or get pushed out of position by ice.

Foam Inserts ($4–$8/ft)

Foam blocks that fill your gutter channel and let water filter through while blocking debris on the surface. The problem? Foam traps moisture, encourages mold and seed growth inside the gutter, and degrades in UV light. We don't recommend these for Cherry Hill homes — our freeze-thaw cycles destroy them quickly.

Reverse Curve / Surface Tension ($8–$18/ft)

These solid covers use surface tension to guide water around a curved lip into the gutter while debris falls off the edge. They work reasonably well for large leaves but struggle with pine needles and small debris. They can also be visible from the ground, which bothers some homeowners. Heavy rain can overwhelm the surface tension design, sending water sheeting past the gutter entirely.

Micro-Mesh Systems ($15–$28/ft)

The premium tier. Systems like GutterGlove use surgical-grade stainless steel micro-mesh with openings as small as 50 microns. They block everything — leaves, pine needles, shingle grit, even pollen clumps — while handling extreme rainfall. These are the only guards we install because they're the only ones that actually eliminate gutter cleaning long-term.

Factors That Affect Your Price

Even within the same guard type, your actual cost depends on several home-specific factors:

1. Home Height and Stories

Single-story ranch homes are the easiest and cheapest to outfit. Two-story colonials — extremely common in Cherry Hill neighborhoods like Erlton and Woodcrest — require taller ladders, more safety equipment, and take longer to install. Expect a 15–25% premium for second-story work.

2. Total Gutter Length

More gutters = higher total cost, but many installers offer better per-foot pricing on larger jobs. A 250-foot home might get a lower per-foot rate than a 120-foot home.

3. Gutter Condition

If your gutters are sagging, pulling away from the fascia, or have damaged sections, they'll need repair before guards can be installed. Minor repairs are often included in the quote. Significant gutter replacement adds $5–$15 per linear foot.

4. Roof Type and Pitch

Steep roof pitches (8/12 and above) require specialized safety equipment and slower installation. Some roof materials — like slate or clay tile — require extra care during installation to avoid damage. Both factors can increase labor costs.

5. Accessibility

Tight lot lines, landscaping close to the house, decks, and fences can all make it harder to position ladders. Homes on corner lots or with wide-open yards are typically easier and faster to complete.

6. Season and Demand

Fall is peak season for gutter guard installation in Cherry Hill. Spring and late winter often have shorter wait times and occasional off-season pricing. If your project isn't urgent, booking in March or April can save money.

The ROI Math: Gutter Guards vs. Ongoing Cleaning

Here's where gutter guards really prove their value. Let's do the math for a typical Cherry Hill home:

Without gutter guards:

  • Professional gutter cleaning: $200–$350 per visit
  • Recommended frequency: 2–3 times per year (spring, fall, and optionally mid-summer)
  • Annual cost: $400–$1,050
  • 10-year cost: $4,000–$10,500

That doesn't account for the cost of water damage from missed cleanings — basement flooding, fascia rot, foundation erosion, and landscape washout. A single water damage incident can cost $2,000–$10,000 to repair.

With micro-mesh gutter guards:

  • One-time installation: $2,250–$5,600
  • Annual maintenance: $0 (occasional rinse with a garden hose if desired)
  • 10-year cost: $2,250–$5,600

Over 10 years, gutter guards save most Cherry Hill homeowners $2,000–$5,000 — and that's before accounting for avoided water damage. Over 20 years with a lifetime-warranty system, the savings roughly double.
