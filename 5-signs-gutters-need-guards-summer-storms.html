5 Signs Your Gutters Need Guards Before Summer Storms

Published March 1, 2024 | By CleanGutters Lighting

Early Warning Signs

Summer storm season is just around the corner. If your gutters are showing these five warning signs, installing gutter guards before the storms arrive could save you from expensive water damage. Don't wait—protect your South Jersey home now.

Sign #1: Frequent Overflow During Moderate Rain

If your gutters overflow during typical spring or early summer rain, they'll fail completely during heavy summer thunderstorms.

What This Means:

  • Gutters are partially clogged with debris
  • System can't handle normal rainfall rates
  • Will definitely fail during 1-2+ inch storm
  • Water damage is inevitable during summer storms

The Risk: When summer thunderstorms bring 1-2 inches of rain in one hour, overflowing gutters direct thousands of gallons down your walls directly to your foundation.

Sign #2: Visible Debris Accumulation in Gutters

If you can see leaves, twigs, or sediment accumulating in your gutters, you're looking at a time bomb waiting for summer storms.

What This Means:

  • Your trees are constantly feeding debris into gutters
  • You need frequent professional cleaning (4-6x/year)
  • Debris will clog gutters during intense rainfall
  • Heavy winds will deposit even more debris during storms

The Risk: Summer storms bring both rain and wind. Wind deposits debris into gutters exactly when you need them most, and then heavy rain overwhelms the system.

Key Insight: If you can visually see debris in your gutters during spring, summer storms will clog them. It's that simple.

Sign #3: Water Staining on Exterior Walls

Dark water staining on your exterior walls during rain shows water is running down the sides of your house—a sign gutters are overflowing.

What This Means:

  • Water overflow is documented by visible staining
  • Existing damage shows system is failing
  • Summer storms will cause significantly more damage
  • Foundation and basement at immediate risk

The Risk: Water staining indicates repeated overflow. During summer storms with 5-10x more rainfall, damage accelerates exponentially. What takes a season to show as staining will appear in days with heavy storm rainfall.

Sign #4: Basement or Crawlspace Moisture

If you notice musty smells, moisture stains, or any evidence of water in your basement or crawlspace, failed gutters are likely the culprit.

What This Means:

  • Water is already infiltrating from gutters
  • Even normal rainfall causes problems
  • Summer storms will cause significant flooding
  • Mold risk grows substantially

The Risk: Existing moisture indicates your foundation is already receiving unwanted water. Summer thunderstorms could cause flooding of 1,000-5,000+ gallons into your basement, resulting in $10,000-$50,000+ in damage.

Sign #5: Sagging Gutters or Visible Damage

Sagging gutters, separated seams, visible rust, or holes indicate your gutter system is failing and needs attention immediately.

What This Means:

  • System is structurally compromised
  • Water can leak through damaged sections
  • Heavy rainfall will exceed drainage capacity
  • Complete failure likely during storms

The Risk: Damaged gutters can't handle any significant rainfall. Summer thunderstorms will likely overwhelm the system completely, causing water backup, overflow, and foundation damage simultaneously.

Do You Have Any of These Signs?

If you identified even one of these warning signs, your home is at risk during summer storm season. The good news: gutter guards solve all five problems.

How Gutter Guards Solve These Issues:

  • Prevent debris accumulation (eliminates clogs)
  • Maintain full water flow even during intense rain
  • Prevent overflow onto exterior walls
  • Protect foundation from water infiltration
  • System works reliably even during storms
