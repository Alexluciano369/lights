Outdoor Lighting for Home Security

Back to Blog

Published: January 12, 2025

Burglars choose homes that offer easy, concealed access. Strategic outdoor lighting eliminates hiding spots and makes your home a less attractive target.

FBI Statistics: Homes with visible security measures (including lighting) are 3x less likely to be burglarized than homes without them.

How Lighting Deters Crime

Elimination of Hiding Spots: Well-lit perimeters remove dark corners where intruders lurk.

Increased Detection Risk: Burglars know lit homes mean higher chance of being seen by neighbors or passersby.

Perception of Occupancy: Automated lighting makes homes appear occupied even when empty.

Surveillance Enhancement: Security cameras perform dramatically better with good lighting.

Strategic Lighting Placement

  • Entry Points: Front door, back door, garage doors—all primary access points
  • Walkways: Paths from street to entry points
  • Driveway: Illuminate vehicles and approach routes
  • Yard Perimeter: Eliminate dark corners in backyard
  • Windows: Ground-level windows benefit from nearby lighting

Permanent Lighting Advantages for Security

Always Ready: Unlike motion lights that can fail, permanent systems are maintenance-free and reliable.

Customizable Schedules: Automatically adjust lighting based on time and season.

Away Mode: Simulate occupancy with randomized patterns when traveling.

Instant Control: Brighten all lights from your phone if you hear something suspicious.

Color Psychology for Security

White or cool blue lighting is most effective for security—it's associated with vigilance and provides excellent visibility for cameras.
