What Are Ice Dams and Why Are They Dangerous?

Ice dams form when heat from your attic melts snow on your roof. The melted water flows down to the colder edges of your roof and refreezes, creating a dam of ice. This ice prevents proper drainage, causing water to back up under your shingles and into your home.

The Cost of Ice Dams: According to the Insurance Institute for Business & Home Safety, ice dam damage claims average $5,000-$20,000 per incident. Many homeowners in Cherry Hill, Voorhees, and Marlton face these issues every winter.

Signs of Ice Dam Damage

  • Icicles hanging from gutters (a warning sign)
  • Water stains on ceilings or walls
  • Sagging gutters filled with ice
  • Ice buildup at roof edges
  • Damaged or missing shingles
  • Mold or mildew in attic spaces

How Gutter Guards Prevent Ice Dams

1. Maintain Proper Water Flow

Clogged gutters filled with leaves and debris trap water, which freezes and contributes to ice dam formation. Quality gutter guards like GutterGlove keep debris out, ensuring water can flow freely even in freezing conditions.

2. Reduce Ice Buildup in Gutters

Clear gutters are less likely to accumulate the standing water that turns into problematic ice. When water can drain properly, there's less material for ice dams to form from.

3. Prevent Gutter Damage

Heavy ice can tear gutters from your home, costing thousands in repairs. Gutter guards help distribute weight more evenly and prevent the debris accumulation that worsens ice-related damage.

4. Allow Heat Tape Effectiveness

If you use heat tape or heating cables, clean gutters protected by guards allow these systems to work properly, preventing ice formation at critical points.

Additional Ice Dam Prevention Strategies

While gutter guards are crucial, combine them with these strategies for maximum protection:

  1. Improve attic insulation – Reduce heat escaping to your roof
  2. Enhance attic ventilation – Keep roof temperature consistent
  3. Seal air leaks – Prevent warm air from entering your attic
  4. Professional inspection – Identify vulnerabilities before winter
  5. Snow removal – Clear heavy snow from roof edges after major storms
Expert Tip: The best time to install gutter guards is in fall, before winter arrives. This gives your system time to prove itself and allows for any adjustments before heavy snow season.

Do gutter guards completely prevent ice dams?

When should I install gutter guards for winter protection?
