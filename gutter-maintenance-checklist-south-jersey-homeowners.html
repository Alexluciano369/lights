South Jersey Gutter Maintenance Checklist

Back to Blog

Published: January 12, 2025

Proper gutter maintenance protects your South Jersey home from thousands in water damage. Here's your complete seasonal checklist.

Spring Maintenance (March-May)

  • Remove winter debris: Clear leaves, twigs, and sediment
  • Check for ice damage: Inspect for separations, cracks, or sagging
  • Test water flow: Run hose to verify proper drainage
  • Inspect downspouts: Ensure water exits away from foundation
  • Check mounting brackets: Tighten any loose hardware
  • Look for rust or corrosion: Especially at seams and joints

Summer Maintenance (June-August)

  • After storms: Check for debris accumulation
  • Monitor during rain: Watch for overflows or improper flow
  • Trim overhanging branches: Reduce debris at source
  • Check for pests: Remove bird nests, wasp nests

Fall Maintenance (September-November)

Most Critical Season: Fall maintenance is essential. South Jersey's deciduous trees drop massive amounts of leaves.
  • Clean early fall: Before peak leaf drop
  • Clean late fall: After trees are bare
  • Inspect before winter: Ensure everything is secure for ice and snow
  • Check pitch: Verify proper slope toward downspouts

Winter Maintenance (December-February)

  • Monitor for ice dams: Large icicles are warning signs
  • Remove snow buildup: Heavy snow can damage gutters
  • Check after thaws: Ensure water is flowing properly

Warning Signs of Problems

  • Water spilling over edges during rain
  • Sagging sections
  • Peeling paint on gutters or siding below
  • Mildew or water stains on siding
  • Pooling water near foundation
  • Erosion in landscaping below gutters
