Why New Castle's Historic Homes Need Gutter Guards

The colonial and 19th-century homes in Old New Castle face a unique challenge: their gutters and downspouts were often retrofitted onto structures never designed for them, and decades of deferred maintenance have left fascia boards, mortar joints, and foundation stone vulnerable to water damage. Add to that New Castle's location on the Delaware River — with persistent humidity, coastal storms, and 45 inches of annual rain — and you have a recipe for serious structural issues.

Even New Castle's newer communities — the 1950s-70s neighborhoods in Wilton and Collins Park, and the more recent developments near I-295 — deal with mature deciduous trees that blanket gutters every autumn. The sycamores, sweetgums, and pin oaks that make New Castle's streets so beautiful are relentless debris producers. GutterGlove changes the equation entirely:

Yes. We serve all neighborhoods in New Castle — from the cobblestone streets and colonial homes of Old New Castle and Battery Park to the suburban communities along Route 9 and Route 273, including Wilton, Swanwyck, Collins Park, and everything east of the Delaware Memorial Bridge approach.

How do Oelo lights perform in New Castle's riverfront humidity?

Oelo permanent LED lights are rated IP68 — fully sealed against moisture, salt air, and condensation.
