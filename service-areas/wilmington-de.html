Why Wilmington Homeowners Need Gutter Guards

New Castle County receives an average of 45 inches of rain and 20 inches of snow per year. That water has to go somewhere — and when your gutters are stuffed with leaves from Wilmington's towering oaks, maples, and sycamores, it goes where you don't want it: over the edge, down your siding, and into your foundation.

Wilmington's older homes are particularly vulnerable. The historic districts around Wawaset Park, Forty Acres, and the Highlands feature homes dating back over a century, with aging mortar joints and original gutter systems that weren't designed to handle decades of deferred maintenance. Even newer developments in Hockessin, Greenville, and North Wilmington deal with heavy leaf and seed-pod debris from surrounding woodlands.
