Why Upper Darby Homes Need Gutter Guards

Upper Darby's housing stock — much of it built between the 1920s and 1960s — features mature tree canopy throughout its residential streets. Towering oaks, maples, and sweet gums line neighborhoods like Drexel Hill and Beverly Hills, dropping enormous volumes of leaves, seed pods, and branches each fall. Combined with Upper Darby's position just west of Philadelphia, where nor'easters and summer thunderstorms deliver concentrated rainfall, unprotected gutters become a serious liability.

The consequences are real for Upper Darby property owners. Clogged gutters cause water to overflow and pool around foundations — particularly problematic for older row homes and twins where lot lines are tight and drainage is shared between properties. In winter, ice dams form in blocked gutters, forcing water under shingles and into ceiling cavities. And fascia board rot accelerates quickly on homes where gutter maintenance has been deferred, leading to expensive repair bills.
