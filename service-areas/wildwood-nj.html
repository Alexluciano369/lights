Wildwood is one of the Jersey Shore's most iconic destinations—a five-mile barrier island famous for its free beaches, the neon-lit boardwalk, Morey's Piers amusement parks, and a Doo-Wop architectural heritage that earned it a spot on the National Register of Historic Places. But beyond the summer crowds and the boardwalk energy, Wildwood is also home to thousands of year-round residents and a massive inventory of vacation rental properties. Whether you own a classic Doo-Wop-era motel conversion on Atlantic Avenue, a modern raised beach house in Wildwood Crest, or a year-round home on the bay side of North Wildwood, your property faces the same relentless coastal forces: salt air that corrodes metal, wind-driven sand that fills every crevice, seasonal storms that test every exterior surface, and months of vacancy when no one's around to catch problems early.

CleanGutters Lighting has been serving South Jersey homeowners since 2009 with two solutions perfectly suited for the shore: GutterGlove micro-mesh gutter guards built with corrosion-resistant materials, and Oelo permanent LED roofline lighting that adds color, personality, and security to your property 365 days a year.

GutterGlove Gutter Guards: Engineered for Wildwood's Shore Conditions

Wildwood's barrier island location means your gutters take a beating from every direction. Salt spray from the Atlantic rides onshore winds and coats metal surfaces daily, accelerating rust and corrosion. Sand blows into gutters during coastal storms, mixing with salt residue to form a heavy, abrasive sludge. Seagulls nest on rooftops and drop debris into open gutters. And during the off-season, when homes sit empty from October through April, clogged gutters overflow unnoticed—rotting fascia, staining siding, and pooling water against foundations.

Our GutterGlove system stops all of it. The micro-mesh is surgical-grade stainless steel—corrosion-proof in salt environments. The frame is anodized aluminum with a sealed oxide layer that resists pitting. Together, they create a gutter guard that thrives in exactly the conditions that destroy cheaper alternatives. Water gets through; sand, salt crust, bird debris, and everything else stays out.

Why Wildwood Homeowners Need GutterGlove

  • Salt air won't touch it — Stainless steel and anodized aluminum are the gold standard for coastal hardware. No rust, no pitting, no degradation.
  • Sand and storm debris blocking — Wildwood's nor'easters and summer thunderstorms drive sand and debris into gutters. Micro-mesh blocks particles as small as shingle grit.
  • Unattended property protection — For the majority of Wildwood homes that sit vacant in winter, GutterGlove ensures gutters stay clear and functional without a property manager climbing ladders after every storm.
  • Rental property maintenance savings — Stop paying for quarterly gutter cleanings. One GutterGlove installation eliminates that recurring expense permanently.

Wildwood is a town that celebrates light. The boardwalk blazes with neon every summer night—the roller coasters at Morey's Piers, the arcade facades, the glow of the Ferris wheel reflecting off the ocean. Your home can tap into that same energy with Oelo permanent LED roofline lighting.

Oelo's slim aluminum track installs permanently along your roofline, and the individually addressable LEDs inside it produce any color you can imagine. Set your Wildwood property to pulse with boardwalk-style neon during summer rental season to attract attention and delight guests. Switch to warm, sophisticated tones for off-season ambiance. Frequently Asked Questions

Are gutter guards necessary on a Wildwood beach home?

Yes—even though Wildwood has fewer trees than inland areas, shore homes face wind-blown sand, salt spray buildup, bird nesting material, and debris carried by nor'easters. Standard gutters clog from these sources and corrode quickly in salt air. GutterGlove's stainless steel micro-mesh and anodized aluminum frame block all of it while resisting coastal corrosion for decades.

Absolutely. Oelo permanent LED lights offer millions of colors and animated patterns—from neon pinks and electric blues reminiscent of Morey's Piers to subtle warm whites for a quieter look.
